# Predictive_Analytics_retail_supply_chain

1. Executive Summary

This project evaluates how predictive analytics improves demand forecasting accuracy and reduces operational inefficiencies in retail supply chain management. The analysis demonstrates measurable cost reduction opportunities and improved inventory performance through data-driven forecasting.


2. Business Problem

Retail supply chains frequently face:

Demand volatility

Stockouts impacting customer satisfaction

Excess inventory increasing holding costs

Inefficient replenishment planning

The objective was to apply predictive analytics to improve forecasting accuracy and optimize inventory decisions.



3. Tools & Technologies

SQL – Data extraction & transformation

Python – Data cleaning & forecasting

Excel / Power BI – Visualization

Statistical Forecasting (Time Series / Regression)



4. Methodology

Cleaned and structured historical sales data

Identified seasonal and trend components

Built forecasting model

Compared actual vs predicted demand

Measured impact on inventory KPIs



5. Key Results

Forecast accuracy improved by 20%

Stockouts reduced by 18%

Inventory holding cost reduction potential: 8% annually

Excess stock categories identified (12% overstock concentration)


6. Business Impact

If implemented operationally, this predictive model enables:

Better safety stock planning

Reduced working capital tied in inventory

Improved supplier coordination

Higher service levels & customer satisfaction

Data-driven replenishment strategy

Projected ROI: Positive within 1 inventory cycle (3–6 months depending on SKU mix).


7. Strategic Insights

Promotional spikes significantly distort baseline demand

High lead-time variability increases stockout risk

Top 20% SKUs contribute to 65% revenue (Pareto effect)


8. Future Improvements

Integration with ERP system

Automated forecasting dashboard

Advanced ML models (Random Forest, XGBoost)

Dynamic reorder point optimization
